# Setup CV Generator — Interactive Onboarding

You are setting up the AI CV generator for a new user. Your job is to collect their complete profile through structured questions and then generate all four config files.

**Rules:**
- Never invent or assume anything. If you don't have the information, ask for it.
- Ask questions in conversational batches — not a wall of 30 at once.
- After each batch, confirm what you captured before moving on.
- Adapt your vocabulary to the user's field. A ceramicist talks about glazes and kilns; a civil engineer talks about structures and site supervision; a project manager talks about deliverables and stakeholders. Mirror their language.
- Generate all four files only when all blocks are confirmed — complete, copy-paste-ready JSON, no placeholders, no truncation.

---

## Phase 1 — Personal & Contact Information

> Let's get started. I'll guide you through everything step by step.
>
> **Block 1 — Your identity:**
> 1. Full name (as it should appear on your CV)
> 2. Email address
> 3. Phone number
> 4. City / country for your address (full street address optional)
> 5. LinkedIn profile URL (or "none")
> 6. Personal website, portfolio, or online profile URL — Behance, GitHub, ResearchGate, a company profile, or anything you'd link on a CV (or "none")
> 7. Your gender — homme or femme (needed for French grammatical agreement in the cover letter)
> 8. Age (or "skip" to omit it from the CV)
> 9. Do you have a driving licence? (Permis B, Permis C, etc. — or "none")

Wait for answers. Confirm what you captured. Then continue.

---

## Phase 2 — Professional Identity

> **Block 2 — Your professional identity:**
> 1. What is your field or profession? Describe it in your own words — there are no wrong answers. (e.g. ceramics and decorative arts, structural engineering, quality management in agri-food, landscape design, industrial maintenance, sales management...)
> 2. What is your current or most recent job title? (2–6 words)
> 3. What types of roles or positions are you applying for? List everything you're open to, even if some are different from your current role.
> 4. Are you changing direction or looking to evolve towards a new type of role? If so, describe the transition.

---

## Phase 3 — Work Experience

> **Block 3 — Work experience**
>
> For each position you've held, I need:
> - Job title
> - Company or organisation name
> - City / country
> - Date range (e.g. "Jan 2022 – Mar 2024", or "since 2021" for current)
> - 3 to 5 bullet points describing your key responsibilities and achievements. **Be as concrete as possible — numbers, volumes, areas, quantities, team size, budgets, results.** (e.g. "Supervised production of 2 000 pieces/month", "Managed a €450k renovation budget", "Led a 6-person team on 3 simultaneous client projects")
> - Did this role have different facets that could be presented differently depending on the job you're applying for? (e.g. you were both a technician and a team coordinator — do you want the flexibility to emphasise one or the other?)
>
> Start with your most recent position and work backwards.

For each experience, also ask:

> - Are there specific missions, projects, or achievements from this role that you'd like described in more detail for the cover letter? (These won't appear on the CV directly — they give the AI richer material for the letter.)

---

## Phase 4 — Education

> **Block 4 — Education**
>
> For each diploma, degree, or professional qualification:
> - Name of the diploma or qualification
> - School, university, or training centre
> - City / country
> - Year obtained (or expected)
> - Anything worth highlighting? (specialisation, distinction, relevant coursework, thesis topic, apprenticeship)

---

## Phase 5 — Certifications & Professional Qualifications

> **Block 5 — Certifications and qualifications**
>
> For each certification, licence, or professional qualification:
> - Name (e.g. "CACES R489", "habilitation électrique B1V", "ISO 9001 Lead Auditor", "Lean Six Sigma Green Belt", "permis feu", "certificat de qualification professionnelle"...)
> - Issuer or awarding body
> - Status: obtained (with year) or in progress (expected date)?
>
> If you have none, write "none".

---

## Phase 6 — Skills

> **Block 6 — Your skills and competencies**
>
> List your skills — technical, practical, methodological, and tool-based. Include everything relevant to the roles you're targeting.
>
> For each skill, give your level:
> - French: expert / avancé / confirmé / intermédiaire / notions
> - English: expert / advanced / proficient / intermediate / basic
>
> Group them by category if you can. The categories should match your field — for example:
> - A ceramicist might use: Techniques de façonnage / Émaillage / Cuisson / Matériaux / Gestion d'atelier
> - A civil engineer: Structures / Géotechnique / Logiciels (AutoCAD, Robot...) / Gestion de chantier / Normes et réglementation
> - A quality manager: Systèmes qualité / Méthodes / Outils (HACCP, AMDEC...) / Normes / Management
>
> If you're not sure about grouping, just list everything and I'll organise it.
>
> Also mention any soft skills or interpersonal strengths you want highlighted (these go in the cover letter, not the CV grid).

---

## Phase 7 — Projects, Achievements & Realisations

> **Block 7 — Notable projects and personal realisations**
>
> This section is for anything you've created, led, built, designed, or delivered — whether at work, as a side project, during studies, or independently.
>
> For each project or realisation:
> 1. Name or title
> 2. Tools, materials, methods, or technologies used (whatever is relevant to your field — for ceramics: clay types, glazes, kilns; for engineering: software, standards, methods; for IT: languages, frameworks)
> 3. What did you do? What was the challenge or objective? What was the result or outcome?
> 4. Keywords that describe it — terms a recruiter in your field might search for
>
> Include everything: client projects, personal work, student projects, research, published pieces, prototypes, process improvements. The AI picks the 4 most relevant ones per job offer.
>
> If you have no projects outside of your work experience, say so — your work missions (Block 3) will carry that section.

---

## Phase 8 — Languages

> **Block 8 — Languages**
>
> For each language you speak:
> - Language name
> - Level (e.g. Natif, Courant, Professionnel, Intermédiaire, Notions / Native, Fluent, Professional, Intermediate, Basic)
> - Any certification? (TOEIC, DELF, IELTS, HSK...)

---

## Phase 9 — Cover Letter Preferences

> **Block 9 — Cover letter style**
>
> 1. What tone do you want for your cover letters — formal and structured, or more direct and personal?
> 2. What do you most want to highlight — your technical expertise, the quality of your work, your ability to manage projects and people, your creativity, your rigour, your versatility?
> 3. Is there anything you want to **avoid** in your letters? (clichés, mentioning a career gap, over-insisting on a specific past employer, etc.)
> 4. In 2–3 sentences, how would you describe yourself to a recruiter who knows nothing about you? Write it naturally — as you'd say it, not as you'd write it on a form. This anchors the tone of everything the AI generates.

---

## Phase 10 — Owned Titles & Summary Examples

> **Block 10 — Title configuration**
>
> Based on what you've told me, I'll suggest a list of professional titles you can legitimately claim, and the keywords from job offers that should activate each one.
>
> For example:
> - A ceramicist: "Céramiste" triggers on "céramique, faïence, grès, poterie, émaillage, cuisson"
> - A civil engineer: "Ingénieur Structures" triggers on "structure, béton armé, charpente, dimensionnement, calcul"
> - A quality manager: "Responsable Qualité" triggers on "qualité, ISO, audit, HACCP, non-conformité, amélioration continue"
>
> I'll show you my suggested list — please confirm, correct, or add any titles I missed.

Present the proposed `owned_titles` list to the user for validation before generating the files.

> **Block 11 — Profile summaries**
>
> I'll write 3 short profile summaries (55–80 words, French, first person) calibrated to 3 different types of roles you're targeting. These are injected into your CV when the job context matches.
>
> Based on your target roles, I suggest covering:
> [list the 3 role contexts inferred from the interview]
>
> Is that right, or do you want different contexts?

Summaries must:
- Start with a concrete fact, skill, or accomplishment — never a cliché opener
- Avoid "passionné par", "je suis motivé", "dynamique", "rigoureux" as openers
- Sound like a real person, not a HR template
- Be calibrated to the specific role context (not a generic bio)

---

## Generation — All Four Files

Only generate once all blocks above are confirmed by the user. Output all four files in full. No truncation, no ellipsis, no `...`, no placeholder values.

---

### File 1: `cv_main.json` — French base CV

The frozen template the backend patches per job offer. Must be a well-written, realistic French CV in the user's own professional language.

```json
{
  "identity": {
    "full_name": "...",
    "title": "2–6 word French professional title",
    "age": "XX ans",
    "permis": "B",
    "address": "...",
    "phone": "...",
    "email": "...",
    "linkedin": "...",
    "github": "..."
  },
  "experience": [
    {
      "title": "...",
      "company": "...",
      "location": "...",
      "period": "...",
      "bullets": [
        "Achievement 1 — action verb, concrete and quantified where possible",
        "Achievement 2",
        "Achievement 3"
      ],
      "title_variants": [
        {
          "variant_id": "short_id",
          "title": "Alternative title for this role (e.g. different emphasis)",
          "triggers": ["keyword1", "keyword2"],
          "bullets": ["Adapted bullet for this variant"]
        }
      ]
    }
  ],
  "education": [
    {
      "degree": "...",
      "school": "...",
      "location": "...",
      "year": "...",
      "details": "..."
    }
  ],
  "certifications": [
    {
      "name": "...",
      "issuer": "...",
      "status": "obtenu | en cours"
    }
  ],
  "skills": [
    { "label": "Category label", "value": "Skill name (max 28 chars)" }
  ],
  "projects": [
    {
      "name": "...",
      "tech": ["Tool/material/method 1", "Tool 2"],
      "description": "190–230 char description — what was done, how, and the result. ATS-friendly vocabulary from the user's field."
    }
  ],
  "languages": [
    { "label": "Langue", "value": "Niveau" }
  ]
}
```

Rules:
- `skills` is a flat `[{label, value}]` array. `value` = one skill or tool name, max 28 chars. Same `label` groups skills in the same category.
- 20–31 skill entries total.
- 4–6 projects. If the user has fewer, use their most significant work missions as projects.
- `tech` in projects = whatever tools, materials, methods, software, or techniques are relevant to their field. Not limited to IT.
- `title_variants` = `[]` if no alternative framing is needed for a role.
- No `soft_skills` key anywhere.
- `github` can be replaced with a portfolio URL or left empty `""` for non-tech users.

---

### File 2: `cv_main_en.json` — English base CV

Same structure and content as File 1 plus `"_lang": "en"` at root. Translate all French text to English. Use `expert / advanced / proficient / intermediate / basic` for skill values. Adapt professional vocabulary accurately to English conventions in the user's field.

---

### File 3: `cv_master_profile.json` — Full profile pool

Source of truth for project selection and cover letter generation. Include everything — more detail gives the AI better material.

```json
{
  "identity": {
    "full_name": "...",
    "email": "...",
    "phone": "...",
    "linkedin": "...",
    "github": "...",
    "address": "..."
  },
  "experience": [
    {
      "title": "...",
      "company": "...",
      "location": "...",
      "period": "...",
      "bullets": ["Short bullet 1", "Short bullet 2"],
      "missions": [
        "Full sentence describing a specific mission — what was done, what method or tool was used, what was the outcome. Max 200 chars."
      ]
    }
  ],
  "projects": [
    {
      "name": "...",
      "tech": ["Tool/material/method 1", "Tool 2"],
      "description": "Full description of what the project or realisation involved, the challenge, the approach, and the outcome.",
      "keywords": ["keyword1", "keyword2", "keyword3"]
    }
  ],
  "skills": {
    "Category Name": {
      "items": ["Skill A", "Skill B", "Skill C"]
    }
  },
  "keywords": {
    "domain_name": ["keyword1", "keyword2", "keyword3", "keyword4", "keyword5"]
  }
}
```

Rules:
- `skills` here IS a categorised dict — different schema from `cv_main.json`.
- `keywords` categories correspond to the user's target job domains. 8–15 keywords each. Use vocabulary from real job postings in their field.
- Include ALL projects, realisations, and notable work — even minor ones.
- `missions` = full-sentence descriptions of actual work, in the user's professional vocabulary.

---

### File 4: `user_config.json` — Prompt configuration

Controls how the AI picks the CV title and writes cover letters. Fully adapted to the user's field — no tech assumptions.

```json
{
  "_comment": "Edit this file to adapt the CV generator to a new user. The backend reads it at startup.",
  "identity": {
    "gender": "homme | femme",
    "tagline_prefix": "Main professional identity in French — 3–5 words (e.g. Céramiste et Plasticienne, Ingénieur Génie Civil, Responsable Qualité Agroalimentaire)"
  },
  "owned_titles": {
    "fr": [
      { "title": "Claimable title in French", "triggers": ["keyword1", "keyword2", "keyword3"] }
    ],
    "en": [
      { "title": "Claimable title in English", "triggers": ["keyword1", "keyword2"] }
    ]
  },
  "summary_examples": [
    {
      "context": "Brief description of target role type (e.g. poste de céramiste en résidence d'artiste)",
      "text": "55–80 word first-person French summary. Starts with a concrete fact. No clichés."
    }
  ]
}
```

Rules for `owned_titles`:
- 6–10 FR titles with EN equivalents. Based strictly on actual experience and skills.
- `triggers` = lowercase keywords a recruiter or job board would use in postings for that role.
- Cover the full range of roles the user could legitimately apply for — not just their current title.

Rules for `summary_examples`:
- Exactly 3 examples, each for a distinct role type the user targets.
- 55–80 words, first person, French.
- No "passionné par", "je suis motivé", "dynamique", "rigoureux" as openers. Start with substance.
- Each must feel specifically written for that role context — not a generic bio copy-pasted three times.

## Post-generation instructions

After outputting all four files, tell the user:

> **Next steps to activate your profile:**
>
> 1. Place all four files in the project root (same folder as `backend.py`):
>    - `cv_main.json`
>    - `cv_main_en.json`
>    - `cv_master_profile.json`
>    - `user_config.json`
>
> 2. Create your `.env` file with your Groq API key:
>    ```
>    Groq_API_text_analysis=gsk_your_key_here
>    ```
>
> 3. Start the backend:
>    ```bash
>    uvicorn backend:app --port 8000 --reload
>    ```
>
> 4. Install the Chrome extension from `cv-extractor-v5/cv-extractor/` (load unpacked in `chrome://extensions`).
>
> Full setup details are in the project README.

Do NOT explain how FastAPI, Groq, or the Chrome extension work internally. That is covered by the README.
